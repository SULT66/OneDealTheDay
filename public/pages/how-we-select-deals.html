OUR METHOD

How we select deals

A big discount label does not automatically make something a good deal. We check the price, the product and the seller before featuring it.

1. Price quality

We compare the current price with the usual price and any history we can verify. If we do not have reliable history, we say so instead of calling it a price drop.

2. Historical value

When price history is available, we compare today's price with recent lows, typical prices and earlier discounts.

3. Customer confidence

We look at the rating, the number of reviews and the quality of that feedback. A high rating based on only a few reviews is not enough.

4. Product usefulness

We ask a basic question: is this product useful, and does it make sense for shoppers right now?

5. Availability and retailer reliability

The product should be in stock from a seller we can identify. Delivery, seller history and return terms can all affect our decision.

6. Freshness

We check prices and availability as close to publication as we can. When possible, the page shows when the deal was last checked.

7. OneDailyDrop Score

The score combines price, discount history, ratings, review volume, popularity and freshness. It helps us compare products, but a person still reviews the final pick.

What we do not promise

We only call something the lowest price when reliable data supports that claim. Always check the retailer's checkout page for the final price.
